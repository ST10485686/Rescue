Frequently Asked Questions

To adopt from us, you must:

  • Be 18 years or older
  • Provide proof of address
  • Have consent from all household members
  • Demonstrate ability to care for the animal financially
  • Complete a home visit (for certain pets)

The adoption process typically takes 3-7 days, depending on:

  • Completeness of your application
  • Availability for home visits
  • The specific pet's requirements
  • Reference check timing

Yes, we accept surrendered animals by appointment. Please:

  • Call ahead to schedule
  • Bring any medical records
  • Provide information about the animal's behavior and history
  • Understand that we prioritize animals in immediate danger

If you find a stray animal:

  1. Ensure your safety first
  2. Check for identification tags
  3. Contact local shelters and vets to report found animal
  4. If safe, contain the animal and call our emergency line
  5. Post on local community groups and lost pet pages

We always appreciate:

  • Financial donations for medical care
  • Quality pet food (both cat and dog)
  • Cleaning supplies (bleach, paper towels, laundry detergent)
  • Pet supplies (toys, beds, collars, leashes)
  • Office supplies and gift cards

  • Comprehensive training for all volunteers
  • Various roles beyond direct animal care
  • Flexible scheduling options
  • Opportunities to learn animal handling skills
  • Support from experienced staff and volunteers
